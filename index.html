Financial Service Compensation Scheme (FSCS) ensures that, the customers are compensated in case of an eventuality of a bank going into default. The FSCS is governed by the rules contained with the Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA) and Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) COMP rulebook. This includes a requirement for firms with more than 5,000 accounts held by eligible claimants to provide Single Customer View (SCV) information in an electronic format which is readily transferable to and compatible with FSCS’s systems within 72 hours of request or default. SCV is defined as ‘a single, consistent view of an eligible claimant’s aggregate protected deposits with the relevant firm’. The FSCS have been clear in their expectation of firms in regards to IFTs. Within the 72 hour period, and prior to generating and sending the SCV file, the FSCS would expect firms to take a rigorous and comprehensive approach to the identification and application of in-flight transactions (initiated prior to default) to those accounts within the SCV file. The FSCS would expect firms to apply all transactions that are known by the firm and that were irrevocable prior to the point of default.

Responsibilities:

  • Involved mainly in the technical analysis of the existing code by which I got exposure to the analytical skills
  • Developing the key changes to accommodate the IFT amount in existing feeds as well as new feeds.
  • Executed unit test plan and ensured the functionality.
